# Empathy Map: Education & Skill Development

**Understanding the Online Learner's Perspective**

---

## Central User: Online Learner

---

## THINKS
*What the learner is thinking internally*

- Which course should I choose from so many options?
- Will this actually give me real skills or just a certificate?
- I need to fit learning into my busy schedule
- I'll probably forget this if I don't use it soon
- Is this course at my level or will it be too hard/easy?
- How can I stay motivated to finish this time?
- Do I really have time for another long course?

---

## SEES
*What the learner observes in their environment*

- Long courses with many uncompleted modules
- Teachers reading slides without engagement
- Too many course options and platforms available
- Certificates being promoted everywhere
- Other learners in communities staying motivated
- Non-interactive, passive learning content
- Platforms that don't adapt to individual levels

---

## SAYS
*Direct quotes and statements from learners*

- "Online courses feel long and hard to finish"
- "I start courses and never complete them"
- "I learn better when things are practical"
- "Most online teachers just read slides"
- "I want bite-sized learning that fits into my day"
- "I wish there was feedback on my progress"
- "Certificates are cool, but I care about real skills"
- "I don't know which course to choose â€” too many options"
- "I get bored quickly without interactive content"
- "I'd like learning that feels more like a challenge or game"
- "Sometimes I just want to learn for fun, not for a grade"
- "Learning communities help me stay motivated"
- "I forget what I learned after a week if I don't use it"
- "Most platforms don't adapt to my level"
- "I'd love a mentor who checks in once in a while"

---

## DOES
*Observable behaviors and actions*

- Starts courses with enthusiasm but drops off midway
- Seeks practical, hands-on learning experiences
- Looks for bite-sized content during breaks in their day
- Joins learning communities for motivation and support
- Searches for interactive and gamified content
- Prioritizes skill development over certificate collection
- Abandons courses that feel too long or boring
- Gravitates toward content that feels like a challenge

---

## FEELS: Pains
*Frustrations, fears, and obstacles*

- **Overwhelmed** by too many course choices and platforms
- **Frustrated** with boring, non-interactive content
- **Discouraged** when courses feel too long to complete
- **Bored** without challenges or game-like elements
- **Demotivated** without feedback on progress
- **Anxious** about choosing the wrong course
- **Defeated** when they can't finish what they started
- **Isolated** without community or mentor support
- **Concerned** that learned skills won't stick

---

## FEELS: Gains
*Desires, hopes, and motivations*

- **Motivated** by challenges and game-like experiences
- **Engaged** when learning feels practical and applicable
- **Supported** through community connections
- **Accomplished** with real skill development (not just certificates)
- **Confident** when content adapts to their level
- **Energized** by interactive, hands-on learning
- **Satisfied** when learning fits into their daily routine
- **Encouraged** by regular feedback and progress tracking
- **Empowered** when they can learn at their own pace

---

## Design Implications

**For Product Development:**
- Create micro-learning modules (5-15 minutes)
- Implement gamification and challenge-based learning
- Build adaptive learning paths based on user level
- Add progress tracking with meaningful feedback
- Foster learning communities within the platform
- Incorporate practical, hands-on projects
- Offer optional mentor/coach check-ins
- Focus on skill mastery over certificate completion
- Design for retention and application, not just consumption
